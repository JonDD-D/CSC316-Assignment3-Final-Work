01 — Introduction
Canada on the Map, Seemingly Unified
From coast to coast, under the same national flag, two distinct population realities are emerging: one growing rapidly, youthful, and diverse; the other aging rapidly and gradually being marginalized.
5.2% — Canada's population growth 2016–2021
#1 among all G7 nations
80% of that growth driven by immigration [1]
02 — Population Baseline
Population Distribution in 2016
Canada has an uneven population distribution. Ontario has over 13 million people, accounting for more than one-third of the country's total; while Prince Edward Island has less than 150,000 people. [1]
Colour intensity = provincial population (2016)

03 — Population Growth
Uneven Growth
The gap has widened over the past five years. Some provinces have experienced rapid growth, while others have almost stagnated. Atlantic Canada has seen slow growth, while Ontario, B.C., and Alberta have been significantly ahead.
Colour = population growth rate 2016→2021
Darker green = faster growth
04 — Economic Opportunity
Income and Opportunity Concentration
The after-tax income indicates where the opportunities lie. The provinces with the fastest growth are also the regions with the highest income, continuously attracting population and immigration inflows.
Colour = median after-tax income · 2021
Darker = higher income · hover for exact figures
05 — Education
High Education Is Also Concentrating
Not only are immigrants concentrating, but the population with a university degree is also gathering in the same set of provinces. These regions are simultaneously driving population and economic growth.
06 — Immigration
Uneven Distribution of Immigrants
From 2016 to 2021, the proportion of immigrants rose, but the differences were significant. Some provinces absorbed more new immigrants, and the gap in population structure further widened.
07 — Aging Population
Places Without Immigration Are Aging
Immigration brings in younger populations. However, in provinces with fewer immigrants, the median age continues to rise, leading to an acceleration of aging.
08 — Conclusion
Two Canadas
The data shows that Canada is diversifying: not only regional differences, but also depending on whether it integrates into the growth system driven by immigration.
